SIG Information

Please check out our current list of SIGs in the table below. We currently hold all meetings on-line mostly using Jitsi.
Some SIG Leaders may choose to use other platforms for their meetings.
Most in person meetings are held at the Scotch Plains Rescue Squad, second floor unless otherwise noted.

Scotch Plains Rescue Squad
1916 Bartle Avenue
Scotch Plains, New Jersey 07076
[map]

Feel free to attend any meeting that interests you or contact the SIG leaders for more information. Anyone interested in discussing problems and issues, please attend the relevant meeting to announce and explain (provide as much detail as possible). One can also send all discussions to the ACGNJ Newsletter editor for inclusion in the next volume. Guests are always welcome. We have WiFi, tables, and chairs, so bring your laptops, tablets, etc. Guests who frequently use the facilities are strongly encouraged to join ACGNJ as members and also to attend other meetings of interest.

Most of the SIG leaders maintain their own SIG web site, where you can get current details on the SIG.

If you don't see a SIG that interests you and wish to start one, please send email to the Board of Directors. You will be invited to attend the next Board of Directors meeting to state your case for the proposed group.

Main Meeting

facilitated by the ACGNJ President

first Friday of the month

8:00pm

Description

The Main Meeting is a general meeting for all computer hobbyists and enthusiasts. There is usually a guest speaker to present a topic of general interest.

Topics are brainstormed and scheduled at the annual club Planning Meeting (open to all members in good standing). The current list can be downloaded here.

Please note there are no Main Meetings during the months of July and August (summer hiatus) and sometimes September (Labor Day Holiday).

Mobile Devices

second Wednesday of the even months

facilitated by Brenda Bell

7:30pm

Description

The Mobile Devices SIG focuses largely on current-generation cellphones and smartphones (such as Blackberry, Android, iPhone) which bridge the gap between basic cell phones and traditional computers, and how they can help you manage and organize your life. Our membership is diverse, ranging from those who have recently acquired their first, basic cellphone to those who develop applications for today's modern smartphones, iPods, and ultraportable computers. While we expect to spend much of our time investigating the built-in features and specialized applications available to modern smartphones, if you bring your basic (or multimedia) cell phone, iPod, or other mobile device with questions on how to use it, where to find applications, or what features they have, we are always happy to help!

The general format for all meetings will be on the order of:

  • Introduction
  • Random Access
  • Main Activity/Activities
  • Beginners' Corner
  • Topic of the Day: Application, Operating System, Device Highlights
  • Developer/Expert Corner
  • Topic for next meeting
  • Any activities to schedule between now and next meeting
  • Close

This is subject to change based on the audience/participant makeup -- I can see this going anywhere from adding half-day courses on basic cell phone operation (for which I expect mostly senior citizens) to workshops (either within the activity or scheduled separately) on using particular operating systems (Blackberry, Android, WebOS, iOS, Windows Mobile, etc.) to discussions of applications and classes of applications (OS-independent/agnostic) and onwards...

History

The inaugural Mobile Device meeting was held on Wednesday, October 13, 2010. The agenda was to meet and greet and plan a path forward, i.e., what attendees were expecting from participating in these meetings. Attendees were encouraged to bring all their ideas, PDAs, fancy phones, etc.

Computerized Investing

second Thursday of the month

facilitated by James Cooper

8:00pm

Description

Members of the Computerized Investing Special Interest Group range from new would-be-buyers of computers to programmers that started by soldering/making their own computers. Investing experience varies from "I'm just getting interested" to those that have been investing for over 40 years. The common denominator is an interest in investing. All are welcome.

The group has a typical attendance of 10 - 15 with about 140 members. Jim has published a very thorough and thoughtful article on the concepts of Personal Criteria Formulas (PCFs) and how to use them for scanning stock charts with the Worden TC2000 charting platform. The concepts, however, are applicable to developing and using scans on any other charting platform. He has graciously given permission to access them for your personal use. Remember, this is his personal property and his permission should not be abused. A link to the document can be found on his website, which is linked below.

History

The previous chairperson, Norm Wiss, facilitated the group until he had to resign for health reasons in October 2006. He was still active in investing with the Best Choice Users Group until he passed away in January 2014.

Web Browsers

third Monday of the month

facilitated by David McRitchie

7:30pm

Description

All web browsers will be considered and examined.

  • Firefox: Customization Notes, Keyword Shortcuts, Tabbed Browsing.
  • Dolphin Browser: Dolphin My Gestures -- David McRitchie.
  • Android Browsers: Android Browsers -- Comparison chart (Dolphin, Firefox, Browser, Opera, Boat).

History

The Web Browsers SIG debuted on Monday, January 15th, 2007 to be an open forum for all Firefox and Mozilla techniques and technologies, to encourage the study and development of web sites of all kinds.

Random Access [ex-Tech Talk]

third Friday of the month

facilitated by President

8:00pm

Description

The intent is to provide the members with Windows-oriented application discussions in both Microsoft and Linux style. The presentation will be directed toward the more heavy technological level of attendee, although newbies are, of course, welcome to attend. The Tech Talk Meeting is a general meeting for all computer hobbyists and enthusiasts. There is usually a guest speaker to present a topic of general interest. Topics are brainstormed and scheduled at the annual club Planning Meeting (open to all members in good standing). The current list can be downloaded here. Depending on when TCF is scheduled, there may not be a Tech Talk meeting in March.

History
